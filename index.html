Milestone Goal: I want to become a computer programmer by 1/1/2026

Where I Started

I have always loved computers and technology. When I was in college at the University of Wisconsin-River Falls, I started working as an IT helpdesk technician. This is where I discovered computer science and programming. I then enrolled in three computer science courses at the next opportunity.

Coming into Capita3 Leader Launch, I already had this goal however I did not have any hard deadlines assocated with my milestones. Without deadlines, loose deadlines would come and go with little progress being made. I had completed a few computer programming online courses, made some computeter programs to automate different aspects of my job, and applied to some IT programming jobs.

What I did not take into account was my mindset. Though I felt like the college and online courses that I had completed had prepared me to be job ready. I never felt like I was a programmer. I now understand that in order to feel like a programmer, I need to join programming commmunities.

Where I am Now

Since utilizing Capita3 materials, I have been able to update my long-term milestone plan with additional tasks to complete to address any knowledge gaps and combat the internal and external barriers that I face. I now see that I need to create a team of experts and cheerleaders to help me in accomplishing my goals within the timeframe that I set.

My leadership and how I view myself has also changed during my time in Leadership Launch. I am starting to fell like I can call myself a programmer. This mindset will help greatly when interviewing, as I will be more confident in myself. I also have a well thought out plan with deadlines that I can execute. Having this plan will help hold myself accountable to reach my goals by the deadline that I set for myself.

What Made the Difference

The biggest difference in my growth as a leader came from facing my fears of joining a community and asking for help. I have always struggled to ask for help, but I now realize that in order to efficiely reach my goal, I will need people in my corner.

My biggest "Aha" moment came from identifying internal barriers and recognizing that we develop strengths or "work arounds" to avoide facing these fears. I have always viewed these work arounds as just strengths but I now see that in order to be the leader that I want to be, I need to also address my concerns.

The impact of facing my fears will allow me to gain a network of people to help me reach my goal in a timely manner. It will also help me identify the best plan and projects to create to give me the best chance at getting hired.
